About The Eyo Festival

A deep historical and cultural heritage of Lagos

Origins of the Eyo Festival

The Eyo Festival, also known as the Adamu Orisha Play, is one of the oldest traditional festivals in Lagos, Nigeria. It is believed to date back to the 19th century and is deeply rooted in Yoruba religious and cultural traditions.

Historically, the festival was performed to guide the soul of a departed Oba (king) or important chief into the afterlife. It represents a transition from physical existence to spiritual continuation, reflecting the Yoruba belief in life after death.

Symbolism of the Eyo Masquerade

The white robes worn by the Eyo masquerades symbolize purity, peace, and respect for the deceased. The wide-brimmed hats hide the faces of the performers, reinforcing the belief that the spirits of ancestors are present during the festival.
