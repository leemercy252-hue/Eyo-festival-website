The Eyo Festival

A Living Heritage of Lagos Island

LAGOS HERITAGE

Introduction: A Festival Like No Other

The Eyo Festival, also known as the Adamu Orisha Play, is one of the most extraordinary cultural celebrations in West Africa.

Deeply rooted in the traditions of Lagos Island, the festival symbolises honour, remembrance, spirituality, and the continuity of Yoruba civilisation.

ROYAL HISTORY

Ancient Origins & Royal Connections

Historical accounts trace the origins of the festival to the ancient Lagos monarchy and Yoruba spiritual traditions.

The ceremony was traditionally performed to honour departed kings, chiefs, and noble ancestors.

SPIRITUALITY

The Spiritual Meaning of Eyo

In Yoruba cosmology, life continues after death. The Eyo Festival reflects this belief through sacred processions and ancestral honouring.

The white garments worn by the masquerades represent purity, peace, and the sacred journey of the soul.

MUSIC & PROCESSION

Music, Drumming & Celebration

Traditional Yoruba drums and chants create the powerful atmosphere that defines the Eyo Festival.

Music, dancing, and ceremonial processions unite communities and preserve cultural identity.

The Eyo Groups

Eyo Adimu

The highest and most sacred Eyo group.

Eyo Oniko

Recognised by yellow ceremonial decorations.

Eyo Ologede

Associated with green banana leaf symbolism.

Eyo Elefon

One of the oldest traditional groups.

MODERN LEGACY

Cultural Importance Today

Today, the Eyo Festival stands as one of the strongest symbols of Lagos identity and Yoruba heritage.

The festival attracts visitors from around the world and continues to influence art, fashion, tourism, and African cultural storytelling.

Did You Know?

Special Occasions

The Eyo Festival is not held every year.

Global Recognition

It is one of Nigeria’s most photographed cultural events.

Sacred Procession

Certain sacred paths must not be crossed during the procession.

Cultural Influence
