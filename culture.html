Yoruba Culture & Traditions

Discover the traditions, beliefs and heritage behind the Eyo Festival

The Yoruba People

The Yoruba people are one of the largest ethnic groups in West Africa, known for their rich traditions, royal heritage, spirituality, language, music, and artistic expression.

Yoruba culture strongly values respect, family unity, storytelling, and community identity across generations.

Music & Drumming

Traditional Yoruba drumming is one of the most important parts of the Eyo Festival celebration.

Talking drums communicate rhythm, praise, history, and ceremonial messages during the procession.

Traditional Attire

The Eyo masquerades wear elegant white garments that symbolize purity, peace, spirituality, and ancestral honour.

Traditional Yoruba clothing such as Agbada, Adire, and Aso-Oke fabrics are worn during ceremonies and festivals.

Religion & Spiritual Beliefs

Yoruba traditional religion is centered around Olodumare, the supreme being, and spiritual deities known as Orishas.

These beliefs influence ceremonies, festivals, royal traditions, and community practices.

Cultural Legacy

Yoruba culture continues to influence music, fashion, film, literature, and art across Africa and around the world.

Festivals like Eyo preserve African heritage and introduce Yoruba traditions to international audiences.

“Culture is the identity of a people, and the Eyo Festival remains one of the strongest symbols of Yoruba heritage.”

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do Eyo masquerades wear white?

White garments symbolize purity, spirituality, peace, and ancestral honour.

Can tourists attend the Eyo Festival?

Yes. Visitors from around the world are welcome to experience the festival.

What makes the Eyo Festival unique?

The festival combines traditional spirituality, royal history, music, dance, and ceremonial processions.
