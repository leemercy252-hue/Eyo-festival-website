The Living Heritage of Eyo Festival

During the Eyo Festival, Lagos Island transforms into a vibrant stage of cultural expression. The streets are filled with white-robed masquerades known as “Eyo,” who move in organised processions to honour tradition, royalty, and the memory of departed kings. Their movements are guided by deep cultural rules and spiritual significance.

Traditional drumming, chants, and songs echo through the city as communities gather to celebrate. Each group of Eyo performers represents different lineages and chieftaincy houses, and their presence reflects respect for ancestry and Yoruba heritage. The atmosphere is both festive and deeply symbolic, blending celebration with spirituality.

Visitors witness a rare cultural experience where history comes alive through performance, costume, and ritual. The festival also includes community gatherings, storytelling, and moments of reverence that remind participants of the importance of unity, respect, and cultural preservation.
