Welcome To Eyo Festival

The Eyo Festival is one of the most iconic and historically significant cultural celebrations in Lagos, Nigeria. Often referred to as the “Adamu Orisha Play,” it represents the deep spiritual and cultural traditions of the Yoruba people. The festival is widely recognised for its striking procession of white-robed masquerades known as “Eyo,” who move through the streets of Lagos in a symbolic display of honour, respect, and ancestral remembrance.

The festival is deeply rooted in symbolism. The white attire worn by participants represents purity and peace, while the staffs carried by the masquerades signify authority and cultural continuity. The rhythmic sound of traditional drums, chants, and songs fills the streets, creating an atmosphere that blends spirituality with celebration.

This website was created to promote and preserve the history, culture, and significance of the Eyo Festival. It aims to educate visitors, celebrate Yoruba traditions, and showcase the beauty of Lagos as a cultural destination. Through this platform, users can explore the history, gallery, tourism information, and key aspects of one of Africa’s most unique cultural festivals.
