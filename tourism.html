Everything you need to attend the Eyo Festival in Lagos

Experience The Eyo Festival Live

The Eyo Festival, also known as the “Adamu Orisha Play,” is one of the most historic cultural celebrations in Lagos, Nigeria. It is a powerful symbol of Yoruba heritage, honouring tradition, spirituality, and community identity.

Every year, the streets of Lagos transform into a living cultural stage filled with drumming, chanting, and the iconic procession of white-robed Eyo masquerades.

About The Festival

The festival is believed to date back several centuries and is traditionally held to honour important figures in Lagos history and to celebrate the legacy of the Yoruba people.

The white attire worn by the Eyo symbolizes purity and respect, while the walking staffs represent authority and ancestral guidance.

Cultural Significance

The Eyo Festival is more than entertainment — it is a spiritual and cultural expression of identity. It connects modern Lagos to its ancestral roots and preserves traditions for future generations.

It is widely recognized as one of the most visually stunning cultural festivals in West Africa, attracting tourists, historians, and cultural enthusiasts from around the world.

How To Attend

  • Register before the festival date
  • Choose your preferred ticket package
  • Arrive early due to large crowds
  • Follow all official instructions
  • Respect cultural traditions

Important Guidelines

  • No disruption of procession routes
  • Respect all masquerades
  • Photography in allowed areas only
  • Modest and respectful dressing required
  • Follow safety officials at all times

Travel & Tourism Information

Lagos offers a wide range of accommodation options including luxury hotels, guest houses, and cultural lodges. Visitors are encouraged to book early due to high demand during festival season.

Popular nearby attractions include Lagos Island heritage sites, traditional markets, and coastal landmarks.

Economic & Tourism Impact

The Eyo Festival contributes significantly to Lagos tourism by attracting international visitors and supporting local businesses. It promotes cultural exchange and strengthens Nigeria’s global cultural identity.
